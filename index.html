Safeguarding: Recognise. Respond. Refer.

Simple, practical guidance to help you identify safeguarding concerns and take the correct steps to keep people safe.

See Action Steps

What is Safeguarding?

Definition

Safeguarding is the process of protecting children and adults from abuse, neglect, and harm. It focuses on prevention, timely action, and appropriate referral.

Principles

  • Empowerment and respect
  • Prevention and proportionate action
  • Protection and partnership
  • Accountability

Who it protects

Anyone at risk of harm, including children, vulnerable adults, and those facing exploitation or abuse.

Your role

Recognise signs, respond calmly, record facts, and report concerns through the proper channels.

How It Works

1

Recognise

Notice signs or disclosures that suggest risk of harm.

2

Respond

Stay calm, reassure, and ensure immediate safety.

3

Refer

Record facts and report via the proper pathway.

Signs to Watch For

  • Physical signs Unexplained injuries, repeated injuries, or signs of neglect.
  • Behavioural signs Sudden withdrawal, fear, or unusual anxiety around a person.
  • Emotional signs Low mood, unexplained anger, or sudden changes in mood.
  • Environmental signs Unsafe living conditions or lack of basic care.

Action Steps

  1. Stay calm: Keep the person safe and listen without judgement.
  2. Record: Note what you observed, dates, times and any direct quotes.
  3. Report: Follow your organisation's reporting pathway or contact local safeguarding services.
  4. Refer: If immediate danger, call emergency services. Otherwise contact the appropriate safeguarding team.

Quick contacts

  • Emergency services (local) 999
  • Safeguarding team (24/7) safeguard@example.org
