What is Safeguarding? ‚ÑπÔ∏è Essential Info

Definition

Safeguarding is the process of protecting children and adults from abuse, neglect, and harm. It focuses on prevention, timely action, and appropriate referral.

Principles

  • Empowerment and respect
  • Prevention and proportionate action
  • Protection and partnership
  • Accountability

Who it protects

Anyone at risk of harm, including children, vulnerable adults, and those facing exploitation or abuse.

Your role

Recognise signs, respond calmly, record facts, and report concerns through the proper channels.

Indicators

Signs to Watch For ‚ö†Ô∏è Watch For

  • Physical signs
    • Unexplained or repeated injuries
    • Signs of neglect or poor hygiene
  • Behavioural signs
    • Withdrawal, fear, or heightened anxiety
    • Unusual secrecy or sudden changes
  • Emotional signs
    • Low mood or unexplained anger
    • Rapid shifts in emotions
  • Environmental signs
    • Unsafe living conditions
    • Lack of basic care or supervision
Action

Action Steps ‚úì Take Action

  1. Stay calm: Keep the person safe and listen without judgement.
  2. Record: Note what you observed, dates, times and any direct quotes.
  3. Report: Follow your organisation's reporting pathway or contact local safeguarding services.
  4. Refer: If immediate danger, call emergency services. Otherwise contact the appropriate safeguarding team.

Do

  • Reassure and ensure immediate safety
  • Record facts exactly, avoid interpretation
  • Refer promptly via proper channels

Don't

  • Promise secrecy or confront suspects
  • Delay reporting or ‚Äúwait and see‚Äù
  • Share sensitive details widely

Examples

Case Examples

School disclosure

A pupil quietly tells you they don‚Äôt feel safe at home.

Action: Stay calm, thank them for telling you, avoid promises of secrecy, ensure immediate safety, record exact words and context, and refer to the safeguarding lead the same day.

Elder neglect

You notice weight loss, poor hygiene, and untreated sores in a neighbor.

Action: Check immediate needs, document observations with dates, and contact local adult safeguarding services. If health risk is urgent, call emergency services.

Online exploitation

A young person receives coercive messages from an unknown adult online.

Action: Preserve evidence (screenshots, usernames), avoid confronting the suspected offender, provide reassurance, and report through safeguarding and appropriate authorities.
